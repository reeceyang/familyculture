Chinese Immmigration

Chinese Americans are the third-largest foreign born group in America. Many of them immigrated for job opportunities. Many of them saved up money to take back home. Others stayed permanently. Some people immigrated to escape political unrest. Americans grew suspicious and dislikeful of the Chinese, and the Chinese Exclusion Act was passed in 1882. In the late 1900s, Chinese Americans began to immigrate for education and afterwards to seek jobs.

Works Cited
